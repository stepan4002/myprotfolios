Case · 01
Csetneki — luxury maison, three-city tour
Štítnik · Prague · Budapest · Rožňava
Multi-city tour · 2025–2027
Role · co-curator & co-organiser

An eleven-look luxury maison built on Štítnik lace — a heritage craft developed by the Szontágh sisters from 1905. Brought together with four artists: textile designer Vlasta Žáková, painters Alexandra Atlasová and Martina Štěpán, and Prof. MUDr. Svatopluk Adámek (UMPRUM Prague). The looks combine historical lace redrawn through digital + machine embroidery, upcycled textiles, ceramic ornaments from the Gemcraft workshop, and a finale on the "fragile inner armour of the feminine."

What I delivered, end-to-end:

  • Curatorial brief — 11-look concept order, designer medallions, runway script.
  • Venues — historic Prague palace setting; indoor production permits in Štítnik / Rožňava; fire-safety sign-off for runway lighting.
  • Artist coordination — contracts, accommodation, transport, riders, payouts.
  • Supply chain — ceramics from Gemcraft workshop, embroidery from Vlasta's atelier, paintings sourced; one-source-of-truth inventory list per look.
  • Press — regional + Prague + Budapest media; one-page release + image bank; embargoes managed per stop.
  • Front-of-house — guest list, RSVP, seat allocation, walk-on cards, photographer brief, hospitality flow.
  • Marketing pack — SK / CZ / EN / HU posters, social cards, runway-order PDF, designer medallions, press kit.

Result. 2025-12-13 teaser show in Štítnik stress-tested the supply chain at hometown scale; 2026-04-22 Prague stop in a historic palace setting; 2026-05 Budapest exhibition; 2026-06-26 official collection launch in the Gemer region; Vienna · Prague · Budapest planned for 2027.

Case · 02
Gastro Gemer Fest — regional food festival
Main public square · Rožňava
5th edition · 27 June 2026
Capacity 500+

A one-day regional food festival on Rožňava's main public square. Five editions in, the festival has become the cross-border anchor for the Gemer gastronomic calendar — Slovak and Hungarian visitors arrive from both sides of the Slaná valley.

Production scope:

  • Municipal public-square permit — sanitation, waste plan, fire/electrical clearance, MoP liaison.
  • Stall map with electrical run, water, queueing flow; 25–40 regional producers recruited.
  • Stage programme — folklore ensembles, live music, cooking demos.
  • Bilingual SK/HU signage from the main road inwards — measurable cross-border footfall lift.
  • Cashless payment partner; one queue plan per stall cluster.
  • Volunteer roster, single WhatsApp group, one printed run-sheet per shift.

What I learned that I'd carry to Budapest. Food events live or die on flow. The stall map + queue plan + cashless partner is half the production. Bilingual signage at the road edge is what actually pulls cross-border guests.

Case · 03
Wine tastings & multi-course suppers
Heritage estate grounds · Betliar
Summer + autumn programme
40–90 covers · curated 6–8 pours

Curated wine tastings on a historical heritage estate, often combined into multi-course suppers with regional gastronomy partners. Designed for premium small groups — corporate clients, cultural-tourism audiences, the Csetneki press circuit on its Rožňava stop.

What goes into each tasting:

  • Sommelier or certified-pour staffing; pre-selected 6–8 wines with bilingual tasting notes.
  • Glassware procurement — count, back-up, on-site cleaning.
  • Cheese & charcuterie pairing — local producer partner.
  • Liquor licensing & responsibility-pour rules.
  • Multi-course supper option — partner kitchens, allergen log, table service plan.
  • Hard capacity cap at 90; we sell out before we oversell.

Why it works. Hard caps protect the experience; bilingual notes (SK + EN, HU on request) let cross-border guests follow the pour at their own pace; the supper add-on raises AOV without thinning the host's attention.

Osudy umenia — roaming art-film festival
Rakovnica · Kobeliarovo · Klaštor Rožňava
Weekend · July 2026
Audience 250–600 across sites

A weekend art-film festival that roams between three small cultural venues in the Gemer region. Short-form documentary, artist talks, screening discussions. A small festival that moves attracts curious audiences from each village along the route — total throughput beats any single-venue equivalent.

What we produced:

  • Line-up with curators; screening rights secured.
  • Three venue contracts; rolling schedule with intentional buffer for audience travel between sites.
  • Mobile projection rig + sound + blackout solution for non-cinema venues.
  • Bilingual host scripts SK / EN; live translation on Q&A.
  • Press release; regional + Bratislava media list; in-house editorial cover from Gemer Times.
  • One WhatsApp group, one printed run-sheet per venue, one FOH lead per site + a connecting runner.

Lesson. Q&A is the actual product. Films are the bait. Projection-rig redundancy matters — every venue is a different room with different acoustics, light leak and power.

Summer Academy — three parallel youth schools
Štítnik (Gemcraft) · Betliar
July–August 2026 · 5-week footprint
80–150 youth · 12+ facilitators

Three parallel youth summer schools — ceramics, drawing/painting, music-theatre — running over a five-week footprint with a joint vernissage final weekend. Operating playbook:

  • Materials kit per child — at 130% buffer; youth always over-consume.
  • Parental waivers + youth-programme insurance + GDPR-compliant photo consent.
  • Three meals + snacks daily; allergen log; catering partner.
  • Daily transport between dormitory and workshop / studio.

Effect on next year's enrolment. Daily photo reports become the marketing — parents share into their networks; the following year fills before public open.
